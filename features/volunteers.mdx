Volunteers are the core personnel records for each event. Every volunteer belongs to a single event, can be assigned to a group, and optionally carries a maximum-hours limit that the Auto-Assign engine respects.

## Volunteer record fields

Each volunteer record stores the following fields:

| Field         | Required | Description |
| :------------ | :------- | :---------- |
| `name`        | Yes      | Full display name shown across the app and in the Kiosk. |
| `group`       | No       | Group affiliation (e.g. `Delegates`, `Adults`, `Medical`). Controls which shifts this volunteer is eligible for. |
| `max_hours`   | No       | Maximum hours this volunteer may be scheduled. Leave blank for unlimited. |
| `email`       | No       | Contact email address. |
| `phone`       | No       | Contact phone number. |
| `external_id` | No       | Optional identifier for mapping volunteers imported from an external system. |

<Info>
  `external_id` is stored in the database schema but is not exposed in the manual-add form. It is only populated via CSV import when your file contains a matching column.
</Info>

## Adding volunteers manually

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Volunteers page">
    Navigate to your event, then click **Volunteers** in the sidebar. The page shows the full personnel directory for that event.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Volunteer">
    Click the **Add Volunteer** button in the top-right toolbar. A form expands below the header.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the details">
    Enter at minimum a **Full Name**. Optionally select a **Group Assignment** from the dropdown, enter a **Phone Number**, an **Email Address**, and a **Max Hours** value.

    The **Max Hours** field accepts decimal steps of `0.5` — for example, `8.5` hours.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click **Confirm Addition**. The volunteer appears at the top of the list immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Inline editing

You can update any volunteer's details without leaving the list view.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Hover over the row">
    Hover over a volunteer row. The **Edit** (pencil) and **Delete** (trash) action buttons appear on the right.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Edit">
    Click the pencil icon. The row expands into an inline edit form with pre-filled fields for name, group, max hours, phone, and email.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save changes">
    Modify any field, then click the green checkmark to save. Click the grey X to discard changes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use the search bar to filter volunteers by name or group before editing. This is faster than scrolling when managing large rosters.
</Tip>

## Removing volunteers

To remove a single volunteer, hover over their row and click the **trash icon**. A confirmation dialog will appear before the record is deleted.

To remove **all** volunteers at once, click the trash icon in the toolbar (top-left of the volunteer list). This action cannot be undone.

<Warning>
  Deleting a volunteer also removes all of their assignments. Shifts they were filling will become unfilled.
</Warning>

## Volunteer groups

Groups categorize volunteers by role (e.g. `Medical`, `Security`, `Delegates`). Groups are color-coded and appear as badges throughout the assignment interface.

You manage groups from the **Manage Groups** page, accessible via the button at the top of the Volunteers page. From there you can create groups with custom colors and rename or delete existing groups.

<Note>
  A volunteer's group must match the `required_groups` setting on a shift for Auto-Assign to place them there. See [Groups](/features/groups) for full details on how group requirements work.
</Note>

## Max hours and Auto-Assign

The **Max Hours** field caps the total hours a volunteer will be scheduled during Auto-Assign.

When Auto-Assign runs, the Go Scheduler API receives each volunteer's `max_hours` value. The engine will not assign a volunteer to a shift if doing so would push their cumulative scheduled hours above that limit. Volunteers with no `max_hours` set are treated as having a limit of `999` hours, effectively unlimited.

If a volunteer cannot be placed because their max hours would be exceeded, the affected shift appears in the API's `conflicts` response with a reason such as:

```
Volunteer vol_7 already reached max_hours (40.0)
```

These conflicts are surfaced in the **Scheduling Insights** panel on the Assignments page.

## Conflict detection

The Assignments page performs real-time conflict checking on the existing schedule. A conflict is flagged when:

- A volunteer is assigned to two shifts whose time windows overlap (`StartA < EndB && StartB < EndA`).
- A volunteer's total assigned hours exceed their `max_hours` limit.

Shifts with conflicts are sorted to the top of the Assignments list and highlighted in yellow. The exact conflict message — including the volunteer's name, hours, and limit — is shown inline on each affected shift card.
